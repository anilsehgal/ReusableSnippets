Drag

Drag is a backward force that limits the speed of an airplane. Drag is caused by any airplane surface that deflects the airflow around the airplane. For example, extended landing gear poses additional drag on a flying airplane because the gear deflects airflow as the airplane moves through the air.

Drag is the force that opposes thrust and limits the forward speed of the airplane. In straight-and-level, unaccelerated flight, drag and thrust are equal.
